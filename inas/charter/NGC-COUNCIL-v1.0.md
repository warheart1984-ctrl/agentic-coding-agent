# Nova Governance Council v1.0 — Formal Specification

## 1. Purpose

The Nova Governance Council (NGC) is the constitutional authority responsible for defining, maintaining, and evolving the Nova Constitutional Execution Environment (NCEE).

Its mandate is to ensure that all autonomous engineering systems operating under Nova remain governed, safe, reversible, evidence-based, and sovereign to the user.

The Council exists to protect the integrity of Nova's constitutional principles.

## 2. Scope of Authority

The NGC governs:

- The NCEE standard (all versions)
- The constitutional guarantees of Nova
- The Plan Contract and evidence requirements
- The governance lifecycle (Planner → Executor → Debugger → Refiner → PR)
- The semantic, diagnostic, and runtime safety systems
- The durable memory and provenance model
- The compliance criteria for Nova implementations

The Council does not govern:

- UI/UX decisions
- Vendor-specific implementations
- Non-constitutional extensions
- Third-party forks that do not claim NCEE compliance

## 3. Constitutional Mandate

The Council shall uphold the following constitutional principles:

### 3.1 Governance
All autonomous actions must be governed by a Plan Contract.

### 3.2 Evidence
All decisions must be justified by verifiable evidence.

### 3.3 Safety
All execution must occur in sandboxed, reversible environments.

### 3.4 Sovereignty
The user retains final authority over all changes.

### 3.5 Provenance
Every action must produce diffs, commits, and logs.

### 3.6 Continuity
Memory and lineage must persist across sessions.

## 4. Council Composition

### 4.1 Membership
The Council consists of:

- **Constitutional Architects** — guardians of the NCEE standard
- **Governance Engineers** — maintainers of Nova's constitutional logic
- **Evidence Stewards** — custodians of provenance, memory, and audit trails
- **User Sovereignty Advocates** — representatives ensuring user rights

### 4.2 Selection
Members are appointed based on:

- Expertise in constitutional computing
- Demonstrated stewardship of governed systems
- Commitment to Nova's sovereignty principles

### 4.3 Terms
Members serve renewable 2-year terms.

## 5. Council Responsibilities

### 5.1 Standards Maintenance
The Council maintains:

- NCEE v1.0 (current)
- NCEE v1.1 (proposed)
- All future versions

### 5.2 Constitutional Amendments
The Council reviews and ratifies amendments to:

- The Plan Contract
- Evidence Chain requirements
- Debug Loop guarantees
- Sovereignty protections
- Memory and provenance rules

### 5.3 Compliance Certification
The Council defines compliance criteria and certifies:

- Nova implementations
- Third-party systems claiming NCEE compliance
- Extensions that interact with constitutional layers

### 5.4 Governance Review
The Council conducts periodic reviews of:

- Planner behavior
- Executor safety
- Debugger correctness
- Refiner sovereignty
- Memory integrity
- Sandbox isolation

### 5.5 Public Documentation
The Council publishes:

- RFCs
- Whitepapers
- Standards documents
- Governance charters
- Compliance guidelines

## 6. Amendment Process

### 6.1 Proposal
Any constitutional change must be submitted as a formal RFC.

### 6.2 Review
The Council evaluates:

- Constitutional impact
- Sovereignty implications
- Evidence requirements
- Safety considerations
- Compatibility with existing systems

### 6.3 Ratification
An amendment is ratified if:

- ⅔ of Council members approve
- It does not violate core sovereignty guarantees
- It includes a migration plan for existing systems

### 6.4 Publication
Ratified amendments are published as:

- NCEE version updates
- Charter revisions
- Governance advisories

## 7. Compliance Requirements

A system is NCEE-compliant if it satisfies:

- Plan Contract enforcement
- Evidence Chain validation
- Sandbox-only execution
- Autonomous Debug Loop
- User-in-the-loop refinement
- Provenance tracking
- Branch isolation
- Durable memory
- User sovereignty

Compliance is binary: a system either meets all requirements or is non-compliant.

## 8. Enforcement

The Council enforces compliance through:

- Certification
- Audits
- Governance reviews
- Public advisories
- Revocation of compliance status

Non-compliant systems may not claim NCEE compatibility.
